Supplier Onboarding

HCPs need: HCP form (fully signed), ID, bank details, medical license. HCOs need: HCO form (fully signed), CR, bank details, VAT certificate. 3rd Party needs: form (stamped + signed), CR, bank details, VAT certificate. Incomplete submissions will not be processed.

No. All required documents must be received, verified, and confirmed as complete before submission to S360. Partial submissions will be returned and delay the onboarding timeline.

An ID is a government-issued identification document required for individual HCP onboarding. A Commercial Registration (CR) is a legal business document required for HCOs and third-party suppliers. Submitting the wrong type will result in rejection.

Medical licensure validates that the individual is legally authorized to practice. HCOs and third parties are organizational entities verified through their CR and VAT documentation instead.

Individual HCPs are typically not VAT-registered entities under applicable regulations. VAT documentation only applies to organizational suppliers (HCOs and third parties) operating as registered commercial entities.

Once a complete submission is made to S360, the approval process is handled by the S360/MDG platform backend team — this is not within the control of the BO or MC. Once approved, the supplier is reflected in AGB within 24 hours. The BO and MC can only follow up and send reminders during this stage.

Suppliers with expired documentation must resubmit updated documents before any new activities or payments can be processed. The requesting team is responsible for flagging upcoming expirations proactively.

Individual licensed medical practitioner → HCP. Hospital, clinic, or medical institution → HCO. Any other commercial entity providing services or goods → Third Party. When in doubt, confirm with the operations team before initiating onboarding.

Purchase Requisition

The IO/CC is the only document required for every activity type without exception. It must be included in every purchase requisition regardless of the nature of the activity.

HON requires: Contract / Honoraria Letter, Besure, IO/CC, and Delivery Date. No quotation, NCC, PRISMA, or Ariba ID is required.

Both require Besure, IO/CC, and Delivery Date. HON uses a standalone Contract or Honoraria Letter. SPON uses a Contract plus a Hospital Letter as a separate required document.

Not a standard contract. Room Rental requires a Hospital Letter in place of a contract, along with Besure, IO/CC, and Delivery Date.

A Quotation is required for: Logistics (MC&E), Promo Materials (ARP), SFDA Bill, and Creative activities. Not required for HON, SPON, Room Rental, or Medical++.

PRISMA is a system-specific requirement applicable exclusively to Creative activity requisitions. If PRISMA approval has not been obtained, the Creative submission cannot be processed.

NCC is required only for Medical++ activity types. It is not applicable to any other category.

The Ariba ID requirement is currently under review for Promo Materials (ARP) and Creative activities. Confirm with the operations team before submission.

The requisition enters the approval workflow. Both BO and MC have visibility on approvers and can monitor and follow up jointly. Once approved, a PO is generated and made available for download. The supplier also receives the PO. No payment or delivery commitments should be made prior to PO issuance.

Payments & Invoices

HON: Fully Signed Agreement + PO. Sponsorship: Fully Signed Agreement + PO + Reconciliation PDF + Invitation Letter. Rental: PO + Invitation Letter only.

Yes. A PO is mandatory for all payment types — HON, Sponsorship, and Rental — without exception. Invoices submitted without a corresponding PO will not be processed.

Sponsorship engagements involve event-based expenditures requiring post-event reconciliation to confirm amounts match what was agreed. HON payments are fixed-fee arrangements and do not require reconciliation.

The Invitation Letter serves as evidence that the event or activity actually took place. It validates the legitimacy of the expenditure and is required for both Rental and Sponsorship payment types.

BO submits docs to MC → MC reviews and validates → MC compiles and submits to FRA → VIM Service Confirmation triggered automatically → BO confirms service delivery (Invoice Approval) → expense processed according to payment terms. This is the final step in the workflow.

The Business Owner must confirm in the system that the service or activity was delivered as agreed before the payment can be cleared. This step requires the BO's direct action and cannot be completed by the MC on their behalf.

Invoices received prior to PO issuance cannot be processed. Inform the supplier that payment will only be initiated once a valid PO is in place. The BO must ensure the PR is submitted and approved before any supplier engagement is finalized.

Once the BO confirms service delivery and invoice approval is complete, the expense is processed according to the agreed payment terms with the supplier. This is the final step in the Payments & Invoices workflow.

Lead Time & Turnaround

A lead time of 3 business days applies to all requests — vendor creation, PO creation, and all other submissions. Do not submit requests on or after the activity date.

Standard turnaround is 3–5 business days from the date a complete submission is received. In rare or complex cases, this may extend to up to 14 days.

Vendor creation takes up to 14 business days from the date a complete submission is received. This excludes vendor verification time, which is handled by a third-party system outside the operations team's control.

All turnaround timelines begin from the moment a fully complete and valid submission is received. If documents are missing or incorrect, the submission is returned and the clock restarts upon resubmission.
